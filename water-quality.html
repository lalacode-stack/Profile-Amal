Water Quality Sampling & Field Monitoring

Performed water quality sampling across multiple states in Malaysia, covering up to 30 monitoring stations per day. This role required strong field coordination, adaptability to diverse site conditions, and strict adherence to Standard Operating Procedures (SOP).

Scope of Work

  • Conducted water sampling at various locations (rivers, lakes, and surface water bodies) based on scheduled monitoring plans across different regions.
  • Operated multiparameter instruments such as Aqua TROLL 500 for in-situ measurements.
  • Used a dedicated turbidity meter for accurate turbidity (NTU) measurements.
  • Recorded and monitored key water quality parameters, including conductivity (uS/cm), salinity (PSU), total dissolved solids (TDS), turbidity (NTU), pH, dissolved oxygen (DO) and DO saturation, temperature (degrees C), and GPS coordinates (latitude and longitude).

Sample Handling & Preservation

  • Handled water samples in accordance with SOP to prevent contamination.
  • Preserved samples under controlled conditions, such as ice box storage for temperature control.
  • Ensured all samples were delivered to the laboratory within 24 hours to maintain sample integrity and data reliability.

Key Responsibilities

  • Performed instrument calibration before and after fieldwork.
  • Ensured accuracy and consistency of recorded data.
  • Managed field data and prepared preliminary reports.
  • Complied with field safety guidelines and best practices.

Achievements

  • Completed sampling assignments across multiple states in Malaysia, demonstrating flexibility and field readiness.
  • Successfully managed up to 30 sampling locations within a single day.
  • Maintained high-quality and reliable datasets for laboratory analysis.
  • Consistently met sample delivery timelines within 24 hours without compromising quality.

Skills

  • Field sampling and environmental monitoring.
  • Sample preservation and handling.
  • Data collection and reporting.
  • Operation of scientific instruments, including multiparameter probe and turbidity meter.
  • Time management, adaptability, and field logistics.
